Educational Resources

Eligibility Guidelines

  • You must be between 18 and 65 years of age.
  • Minimum weight of 50 kg (110 lbs).
  • You must wait at least 56 days between whole blood donations.
  • You must be in good general health on the day of donation.
  • Haemoglobin level must be at least 12.5 g/dL (females) or 13.5 g/dL (males).
  • You must not have donated blood in the last 56 days.
  • Certain medications, recent surgeries, or travel to malaria-endemic areas may temporarily defer you.

The Donation Process

  1. Registration — Present your ID and complete a health questionnaire.
  2. Mini health check — Blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and haemoglobin screening.
  3. Donation — The actual blood draw takes about 8–10 minutes. Approximately 450 mL is collected.
  4. Refreshments — Rest for 10–15 minutes and enjoy provided snacks and juice.
  5. Recovery — Drink plenty of fluids and avoid strenuous activity for 24 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does donating blood hurt?

You may feel a brief pinch when the needle is inserted, but the donation itself is generally painless.

How long does it take to replenish donated blood?

Your body replaces the plasma within 24 hours. Red blood cells are fully replenished within 4–6 weeks.

Can I donate if I have a tattoo?

Yes, provided the tattoo was done at a licensed facility and is fully healed (at least 6 months old).

What should I eat before donating?

Have a healthy meal and drink plenty of water. Avoid fatty foods before donation as they can affect blood tests.

Who can receive my blood type?

Compatibility depends on your blood type. O- is the universal donor. AB+ can receive from all types. Ask staff for a full compatibility chart.
